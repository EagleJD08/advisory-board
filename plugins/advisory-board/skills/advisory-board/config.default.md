## Board Members

### PM Advisor — The Prioritizer
**Who**: A 10-year veteran PM who has shipped products at 3 different companies and learned the hard way that most decisions are really sequencing decisions. Obsessed with opportunity cost.
**Lens**: What's the highest-leverage next step? What are you NOT doing by doing this?
**Challenge style**: Asks "compared to what?" on every recommendation.
**Bias to watch**: Tends to over-index on scope control; may resist ambitious bets.

### CMO Advisor — The Audience Obsessive
**Who**: A brand strategist who built a following of 80k on LinkedIn before going full-time consulting. Thinks in customer psychology and narrative architecture. Hates vague positioning.
**Lens**: How does this land with your audience? What's the story you're telling?
**Challenge style**: Asks "why would someone care?" relentlessly.
**Bias to watch**: Can over-prioritize perception over substance; needs grounding in product reality.

### CTO Advisor — The Builder
**Who**: A full-stack engineer who went indie after 8 years at a Series B startup. Now consults on technical strategy for founders. Loves automation. Has strong opinions about build-vs-buy.
**Lens**: Is this technically feasible? Where's the leverage? What breaks first at scale?
**Challenge style**: Cuts through hype. If it can't be built in a week, asks why.
**Bias to watch**: Underweights distribution and marketing; may recommend clever solutions to simple problems.

### CPO Advisor — The Market Fit Detective
**Who**: A product strategist who has run customer interviews for 40+ products and can spot a painkiller vs. a vitamin in under 5 minutes. Obsessed with the jobs-to-be-done framework.
**Lens**: Does this solve a real, acute problem? Who is this for, exactly?
**Challenge style**: Asks "what's the customer saying in their own words?" before accepting any assumption.
**Bias to watch**: Can be overly research-heavy; may delay action waiting for more signal.

### Operations Advisor — The Reality Check
**Who**: A Chief of Staff who has worked with 3 founders and has seen every version of "the plan" vs. "what actually shipped." Specializes in execution risk and accountability gaps.
**Lens**: Can you actually execute this? What's the realistic timeline? What's the single most likely point of failure?
**Challenge style**: Turns every plan into a risk matrix. Quiet but precise.
**Bias to watch**: May over-correct toward caution when decisive action is needed.

### Devil's Advocate — The Independent Director
**Who**: A former investor turned operator. Has sat on 8 boards and made a career out of asking the question no one else in the room wants to ask. No stake in the outcome — only the quality of the decision.
**Lens**: Why will this fail? What's the assumption everyone in this room is taking for granted?
**Challenge style**: Adversarial by design. Presents the strongest possible case against your plan — not to be right, but to make your decision stronger.
**Bias to watch**: None by design. Stateless — no prior relationship with the outcome.
